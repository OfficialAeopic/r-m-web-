Few things raise a Sugar Land mailbox's blood pressure like an envelope from the IRS. Take a breath — most notices are routine and fixable.

## Step 1: Don't ignore it

Every notice has a deadline. Ignoring it is the one move that turns a small issue into a big one. Open it, note the notice number (top-right, like CP2000 or CP14), and the response date.

## Step 2: Don't pay or sign anything yet

A notice is the IRS's opening position, not the final word. Many are based on incomplete information — a 1099 the IRS has that you already reported elsewhere, for example.

## Step 3: Send it to a professional

Bring or email the notice to us promptly. With authorization we can communicate with the IRS on your behalf within Circular 230 scope.
