Pearland is one of the fastest-growing communities around Houston, and new businesses launch here constantly — from Shadow Creek Ranch to Silverlake. A question every founder eventually asks: LLC or S-Corp?

## They are not the same kind of thing

An **LLC** is a legal entity formed under Texas law — it handles liability. An **S-Corp** is a tax election with the IRS — it handles how your business income is taxed. You can be an LLC taxed as an S-Corp; the two are layered, not either/or.

## The self-employment tax angle

Without an S-Corp election, an LLC's profit is generally subject to self-employment tax. With a reasonable-salary S-Corp election, some profit can be taken as distributions outside self-employment tax — but only if it is done correctly, with real payroll.
