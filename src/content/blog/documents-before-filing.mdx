Tax season runs smoothly when you have your paperwork organized before you sit down. Here are the five categories of documents we ask every new client to gather before our first appointment.

## 1. Income statements

For most filers, that is your **W-2** from each employer you worked for during the year. If you did contract work, picked up gig income, or earned interest or dividends, you should also have **1099-NEC**, **1099-INT**, **1099-DIV**, or **1099-MISC** forms. If a payer has not sent you one yet, follow up — they are due to you by January 31.

## 2. Deduction-supporting paperwork

If you itemize, gather mortgage interest statements (**1098**), property tax bills, medical expense receipts, and charitable contribution records. Even if you typically take the standard deduction, holding onto these for the year is the only way to know which approach saves you the most.

## 3. Prior-year tax return

A copy of last year's return is one of the most useful documents you can bring. It speeds up onboarding with a new preparer and helps catch carryforward items like capital losses or unused credits that might otherwise be missed.

## 4. Identification and dependent information

You will need a government-issued ID for yourself and your spouse, plus Social Security numbers for any dependents you claim. If you have a new dependent this year — a child born or adopted, or an elderly parent you are now supporting — bring the documentation.

## 5. Bank info for direct deposit

If you want your refund deposited electronically (the fastest option), bring a voided check or your bank's routing and account numbers. We will never store this — it goes directly on your return.

Organize these into a single folder or envelope before your appointment and you will save us both time. If you are missing something, let us know — we will help you track it down.
