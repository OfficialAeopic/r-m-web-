An ITIN — Individual Taxpayer Identification Number — lets people who are not eligible for a Social Security number still file a U.S. tax return. Across Houston's diverse neighborhoods, we help families navigate this every year.

## Who needs one

You may need an ITIN if you have a U.S. filing requirement but are not eligible for an SSN, or if your spouse or a dependent needs to be claimed on your return. ITINs also expire, so renewals are common.

## How Form W-7 works

The ITIN application (Form W-7) is usually filed **with** your federal tax return, along with documents proving identity and foreign status. The most common reason applications get rejected is incomplete documentation — which is exactly what we review up front.

## You may not need to mail your passport

Many applicants worry about mailing original documents. Depending on your situation, certified copies or an IRS-authorized route may let you keep your originals. We will walk you through the safest option.
