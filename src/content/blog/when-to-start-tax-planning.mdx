We get this question a lot in March and April: *"What can I do to lower my taxes this year?"*

The honest answer is: very little, by then. Most planning opportunities open and close earlier in the year. Here is a rough timeline of when the work actually happens — and when there is still time to act.

## January through March — last-call window

A few things remain available even after the calendar year closes:

- **Traditional IRA contributions** can still be made until the tax filing deadline.
- **HSA contributions** for the prior year are open until the filing deadline.
- **Self-employed retirement plans** (SEP, Solo 401k) have extended deadlines tied to your return.

These are the only meaningful moves left. Everything else — retirement plan elections, charitable bunching, capital loss harvesting, estimated payments — needed to happen earlier.

## April through December — the actual planning year

This is when real planning happens. Quarterly check-ins to review estimated payments. Mid-year reviews to confirm withholdings are on track. End-of-year decisions about Roth conversions, charitable giving, and timing of large purchases or sales.

For business owners, the windows are even tighter: payroll structure, entity elections, and equipment depreciation all need to be set up before transactions happen, not reconstructed afterward.

## What we do for ongoing planning clients

We schedule a mid-year touchpoint (usually June or July) and a year-end touchpoint (October or November). At each, we review what has changed — new job, new dependent, big purchase, business changes — and adjust the plan. The goal is no surprises in April.
